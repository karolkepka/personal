Situation: dense binary captures from shock trials → governed analytics

Teams upload MarecX-style payloads at high sampling rates. The gap was not raw storage but trustworthy decoding, channel-aware filtering, and repeatable hull-impact measures — with NATO and University of Gothenburg stakeholders expecting an auditable path from ingest to dashboard.

Cognito JWT API GW → Lambda S3 → Parquet lake Glue + Athena Superset @ EKS
  • High-frequency angle — sparse multi-channel rows (ts, c1…) land in partitioned Parquet; SQS batching, row-count guards, and Athena refresh patterns kept large trials stable.
  • Platform KPIs — KMS encryption; raw vs curated buckets; Glue layers (sensor / filtered / impacts); Athena workgroup with encrypted outputs; pipeline DLQ; Superset on EKS with Cognito-oriented auth settings.
Delivery & management

Scope & contracting — workshops with researchers and sponsors; refined acceptance criteria; aligned statement of work with Crayon; effort sizing across ingest, algorithms, and BI dependencies.

Execution — milestone planning, RAID-style risk tracking, stakeholder cadence, and estimation updates as trial formats evolved.

Hands-on engineering

Core delivery — owned pipeline logic in mashien-algos: binary unpack → validation → calibration-aware measures → curated tables feeding analytics.

Platform collaboration — paired on IaC for Lambdas, queues, Glue/Athena paths (see git footprint on the next slide’s companion repo notes).
